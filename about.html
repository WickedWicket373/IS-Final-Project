About me

Hi, I'm Jacob.

I'm a Computer Science student at Brigham Young University with a minor in Entrepreneurship, on track to graduate in 2029. I came to CS because I've always been drawn to building things — the idea that you can start with a blank file and end up with something that actually does something useful is pretty hard to beat.

Right now I work as a Teaching Assistant for CS 452, which has been one of the better learning experiences I've had. Explaining concepts to other people forces you to actually understand them, and finding clearer ways to present material is something I genuinely enjoy working on.

Outside of school, I'm usually either at the gym lifting, at a climbing gym working on problems, or up at Solitude snowboarding whenever the conditions are good. I'm an Eagle Scout, which taught me a lot about setting goals and seeing them through — something that carries over pretty directly into how I approach both school and coding.

I'm interested in the intersection of CS and entrepreneurship — specifically in how good software gets built into things people actually want to use. Still figuring out exactly where I want to take that, but I'm in the right place to find out.
