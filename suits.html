What is Suits?

Suits is a legal drama that ran on USA Network from 2011 to 2019. It follows Harvey Specter, a hotshot New York lawyer, and Mike Ross, a college dropout with a photographic memory who gets hired as his associate even though he never went to law school. The whole show is basically about keeping that secret while also winning every case that comes through the door.

It hit Netflix in 2023 and immediately went to number one globally, which makes sense because once you start watching it's really hard to stop.

"I don't play the odds, I play the man." — Harvey Specter

Manhattan — where the show takes place

Main Characters

Here are the characters that make the show worth watching:

Harvey Specter

Senior partner at the firm. Brilliant, confident, and the best closer in New York. Played by Gabriel Macht.

Mike Ross

Has a photographic memory and a law degree he never earned. The heart of the show. Played by Patrick J. Adams.

Donna Paulsen

Harvey's secretary (later COO) who somehow always knows everything before anyone else does. Played by Sarah Rafferty.

Jessica Pearson

Managing partner and the most powerful person in every room she walks into. Played by Gina Torres.

Louis Litt

Senior partner who is annoying but also secretly the funniest and most relatable character on the show. Played by Rick Hoffman.

Rachel Zane

Paralegal who is smarter than most of the lawyers. Mike's love interest and eventual partner. Played by Meghan Markle.

Why You Should Watch It

Here are my top reasons, in order of importance:

  1. The dialogue is really sharp
    • Every scene feels like a chess match
    • Characters talk around each other as much as to each other
    • Quotable lines in basically every episode
  2. The style and aesthetic
    • Harvey's suits are genuinely aspirational
    • The offices look incredible
    • The whole show just looks expensive
  3. The loyalty storylines actually hit
    • Harvey and Mike's friendship is one of the best on TV
    • Donna and Harvey's slow burn is worth the wait
    • Even Louis gets a real arc that pays off
  4. It is incredibly bingeable
    • Cliffhangers at the end of almost every episode
    • 9 seasons means you won't run out of content
    • Became the #1 show on Netflix when it was added in 2023
